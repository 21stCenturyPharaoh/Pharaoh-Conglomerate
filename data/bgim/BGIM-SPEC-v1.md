1. USAID / DEVELOPMENT AGENCIES FRAME

Program Title

Community-Embedded Environmental Security & Vector Control System (CEES-VCS)

---

Problem Statement

Urban and peri-urban zones in Monrovia face overlapping risks:

Plastic pollution and blocked drainage systems

Vector-borne diseases (malaria, dengue) linked to standing water

Weak surveillance of illicit activities (poaching, trafficking corridors)

Youth underemployment


---

Intervention

A multi-layer system combining:

A. Waste-to-Resource Infrastructure

Plastic collection and conversion into usable materials

Drainage clearance to eliminate mosquito breeding sites


---

B. AI-Enabled Environmental Monitoring

Acoustic and motion sensors

Community-based alert systems

Detection of illegal activities and environmental anomalies


---

C. Integrated Vector & Pest Control Layer

Evidence-based ecological control methods:

Bacillus thuringiensis israelensis (BTI) for targeted larval suppression

Introduction of larvivorous fish in controlled water bodies

Habitat support for natural predators such as:

bat populations

Insectivorous birds (non-invasive attraction via habitat structures, not wildlife coercion)


This is ecosystem support!

---

D. Coordinated Patrol System (Human + K-9 + Avian Observation)

Deployment of trained domestic working dogs such as the Africanis for:

Scent tracking

Perimeter patrol

Illegal activity detection

Combined with:

Human patrol teams (PLP volunteers)

Sensor alerts

Observational bird activity patterns (passive ecological indicators)



---

Expected Outcomes

Reduction in mosquito breeding sites and vector density

Increased early detection of illegal activities

Youth workforce development

Improved public health indicators
